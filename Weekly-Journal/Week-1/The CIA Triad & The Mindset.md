#### **1. The CIA Triad: The Three Pillars of Security**

This doesn't stand for the government agency. It's the core model:

- **Confidentiality:** Ensuring that information is accessible **only** to those authorized to have access. It's about secrecy and privacy.
    
    - _Example:_ A password protecting your email, or encryption scrambling your online bank details.
        
- **Integrity:** Maintaining the accuracy, completeness, and trustworthiness of data and systems. Data should not be altered in an unauthorized manner.
    
    - _Example:_ A hacker cannot change your bank balance, or a software update cannot be tampered with mid-download.
        
- **Availability:** Ensuring that information and systems are accessible and usable by authorized users whenever they need it.
    
    - _Example:_ Defending against a Denial-of-Service (DoS) attack that tries to crash a website so no one can use it.
        

**Think of it this way:** Your online bank account needs **Confidentiality** (only you see your data), **Integrity** (your balance is correct and transactions can't be altered), and **Availability** (you can log in when you need to pay bills).

#### **2. The Cybersecurity Mindset: "Think Like an Attacker"**

Security isn't just about building walls; it's about understanding why someone would want to break them. Start asking:

- What is valuable here? (Data, money, computing power, reputation?)
    
- How could someone get to it?
    
- What would stop them?
    

This proactive, skeptical thinking is your most important tool.

**Your Practical Takeaway for Today:** Apply the CIA triad to your own life. Look at your email account. How is **Confidentiality** (your password, 2-factor authentication), **Integrity** (can someone alter your sent emails?), and **Availability** (what if you forgot your password?) being managed?
